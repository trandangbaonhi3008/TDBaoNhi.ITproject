About Business Analyst's job advertisement

The Business Analyst will work with organization to help them improve processes and systems. They also take the responsibility as messenger to a clients and company since they define requirements and these systems, then design solutions along with team of developers to address business needs. Business Analyst will be self-oriented, disciplines, accountable and assertive with the attention on delivering solutions that meet our user’s needs. This person required to have good vision about future targets orientation for the company and clients. Technical Business Analyst will join business analysis in different departments of company, the BA may be considered as a technical job role and often stay with IT department. Nowadays, the world is changing with the fast pace, solution of clients is often involve in the implementation of news, improvement, computer system and their roles is to familiarize the wider business with the benefits of the latest technologies so the Business Analyst mindset have to catch up with the trend and be always able to learn new things. None of the day BA go to work are the same, the job require worker to adapt, improve and set a well-achieved targets.

“The business analyst understands the customer’s business requirements and business process management and then translates them to particular software requirements. The position is part of our technology team with the primary function of delivery of high profile initiatives. The business analyst will have the opportunity to have an impact on the technology that is needed to power the organization’s primary business systems. This individual serves the product owners of each business department and reports directly to the Vice President of Technology.”

    Experience

    1. Minimum of 2 years experience in a B/A role with a background in delivering software requirements and managing product development
    2. Experience with JIRA is desired
    3. Strong analytical and product management skills required, including a thorough understanding of how to interpret customer business needs and translate them into application and operational requirements.
    4. A technical IT business analyst must have knowledge of particular programming language or more, accomplish with development skills (like Agile or Waterfall model)


      5. *Agile: is one of the well-known concepts of software development, or also known as an incremental model. This model assumes that the end needs of users are always changing in content of a dynamic business and IT world.

    Soft – behavioral skills

    1. Serves as the conduit between the customer community (internal and external customers) and the software development team through which requirements flow.
    2. Excellent verbal and written communication skills and the ability to interact professionally with a diverse group; executives, managers, and subject matter experts.
    3. Can work independently as an individual and cooperatively with the team as well.
    4. Ability to work independently and to self-direct one’s activities is essential
    5. Strong drive and ability to meet or exceed established deadlines and timetables
    6. Work independently with users to define concepts and under direction of project managers.
    7. Proactively communicate and collaborate with external/internal customers to analyze information needs, functional requirements and deliver the following artifacts as needed: Functional requirements (Business Requirements Document), iii. Use Cases, GUI, Screen, and Interface design.

    Other core skills

    1. Solid understanding of user experience and usability principles
    2. Elicit requirements using interviews, document analysis, requirements workshops, surveys, business process descriptions, use cases, scenarios, business analysis, task, and workflow analysis.
    3. Critically evaluate information gathered from multiple sources, reconcile conflicts, decompose high-level information into details, abstract up from low-level information to a general understanding, and distinguish user requests from the underlying true needs.

    4. Knowledge of database modeling (relational and multi-dimensional) and ETL flows.
    5. Possess a professional skills and expertise in fact-finding techniques to discover or achieve a better understanding of the requirements of the system (requirement discorvery process)
    6. Develop requirements specifications according to standard templates, using natural language.
    7. Ability to work independently with users to define concepts and under direction of project managers.
    8. Strong analytical and product management skills required, including a thorough understanding of how to interpret customer business needs and translate them into application and operational requirements.
    9. Capability of making collaboration with developers and subject matter experts to establish the technical vision and analyze tradeoffs between usability and performance needs.
